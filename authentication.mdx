## Overview

OCCode uses secure token-based authentication through the `occ.mba.sh` cloud service. There are two ways to sign in:

| Method | How it works |
|--------|------|
| **Google** | One-click sign-in — recommended for most users |
| **Email + Password** | Traditional sign-up with your email and a password |

Both methods are equally secure. Google sign-in is just faster.

## How Sign-In Works

Here's what happens when you sign in from OCCode:

<Steps>
  <Step title="OCCode opens your browser">
    The app opens a secure sign-in page at `occ.mba.sh`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="You sign in">
    Choose Google or enter your email + password.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Browser redirects back">
    After signing in, the browser sends you back to OCCode automatically using a secure link (`occode://openclaw.home/auth`).
  </Step>
  <Step title="You're signed in">
    OCCode stores your session locally. You won't need to sign in again unless you explicitly log out.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  The `occode://openclaw.home` link is a custom protocol registered by the OCCode app. It's how the browser securely hands your session back to the desktop app.
</Info>

## Google Sign-In

The fastest way to get started. Click "Continue with Google," pick your Google account, and you're in. Your name and profile picture are imported automatically.

## Email + Password

For users who prefer not to use Google:

1. **Sign up** with your email and a password (minimum 8 characters)
2. **Log in** with the same credentials anytime

<Warning>
  There is currently no password reset flow. If you forget your password, you can sign in with Google instead — if you use the same email address, the accounts are linked automatically.
</Warning>

## Managing Your Account

Visit the [dashboard](https://occ.mba.sh/dashboard) to see your account details, including:

- Your name and email
- Credit balance
- Usage history

To **sign out**, click the sign-out button in the dashboard sidebar or within OCCode.

## Security

- Passwords are hashed with **bcrypt** — we never store your plain-text password
- Sessions use **JWT tokens** sent over HTTPS
- All communication between OCCode and the cloud service is encrypted
